The exposure ruler

Your thumb is the dial.

Drag anywhere along the ruler and the stops glide under a fixed brass needle. In S it drives shutter speed, from Bulb to 1/2000. In A, exposure compensation from −3.0 to +3.0 EV.

The reading turns brass while you move. It turns amber the moment the light runs out and ISO hits its ceiling, no silent correction, no invisible fix. You know when you are at the edge.

The great photographers worked by feel. Winogrand on a New York sidewalk never looked down at a meter. He had calibrated his hands. basic gives your hands the same calibration on glass.

On seeing

The photograph is not made at the moment of pressing the shutter. It is made in the fractions of a second before, when the eye and the hand and the light align. basic removes every obstacle between that alignment and the frame.

On priority modes

Shutter-priority is native to iPhone and honest. You set the ceiling; the sensor finds the light. Aperture-priority is exposure intent, you set the depth of field in mind, the system finds the exposure. Two decisions. Both yours.

On the file

basic shoots JPEGs in Large (~48MP sensor escalation) or Small (~12MP virtual preview) sizes. The live look is baked in. In A-mode, the JPEG embeds native depth map details for focus control after the shot.

The JPEG Philosophy

A photograph is a record of a moment, not a database of raw sensor data. We believe in committing to the shot when you press the shutter. By choosing JPEG, basic focuses on direct, authentic color, instant capture speeds, and active looks that are baked straight into the file. No heavy raw editing pipelines. No massive storage footprints. Just a finished, honest image that captures the light exactly as you saw it.

Looks render live

Compose in the look.

basic is a colour camera. The viewfinder shows true colour by default, Neutral. Mono is a choice, not the identity. The look renders live and is baked directly into the saved JPEG in both Large and Small modes. Swipe down on the frame to cycle looks; the Action Button toggles without moving the phone.

Aperture-priority, honestly

iPhone has a fixed aperture. We say so.

In A-mode, the aperture is locked at ƒ1.4 for maximum computational depth collection, while the dial controls exposure target bias from −3.0 to +3.0 EV in ⅓ stops. The physical fixed aperture of the lens is preserved in the EXIF metadata. Capturing in A-mode commits to a computational JPEG lane that embeds the native depth map and portrait effects matte—allowing full focus control after the shot.

No surprises, and nothing hidden behind a settings screen, because there isn't one. The camera that earns your trust does not hide what it cannot do.
